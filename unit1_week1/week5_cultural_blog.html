Women In Tech

"The tech industry has a well-documented pipeline problem, one largely the result of gender stereotypes that reach into the educational system."
-Soraya Chemaly

Women are under-represented in all tech fields, from start-ups, to programmers, to management-level positions. What's the problem, and does it have an answer?

A few months ago I attended a JavaScript meet-up. The topic was high performance JavaScript, which promised to be an exciting learning opportunity. I've only just begun my long journey into coding/development, but I try to attend talks that are way over my head in order to then go home and research everything I didn't quite understand. This particular talk was huge; the event was at capacity, and about 80-90 people showed up.

The first thing I did when I showed up was to look for a seat somewhere in the middle of the room. I HATE sitting at the back of a room for a presention, and all the spaces at the front had been taken. I saw some empty seats, but I didn't pick one at random, because the second thing I did was to try to spot another woman. Some people might find this to be ridiculous - why would you look for another women when picking your seat? It's only an hour talk, does it matter that much?

The truth is: it matters to me. I'm frequently the ONLY woman at technical meet-ups, and so I always look for other women before I sit down. There aren't as many of us as I'd like there to be, so when I spot a woman I like to sit next to her and ask her questions about her field: what is she working on, where does she work, what are her tech interests? Of course I'll talk to men as well, and I have no discomfort with men, but it's different speaking with someone that is also as keenly aware as you are that you're in the drastic minority.

At this JS talk I picked a seat right in front of a woman, and then proceeded to amiably chat with her and the guy next to me about our JS experience before the presentation started. While talking, I mentioned that I was going to attend DBC. The man in front of me heard this, and turned around. He said, and I quote verbatim, "You're learning to be a developer? I thought you were a recruiter. I mean, you're pretty, and you're friendly, so I just assumed."

Whoa. I'm not gonna lie, I wasn't offended enough, or comfortable enough, to go off on him. And I fully understand that he wasn't trying to be mean or offensive. BUT COME THE FUCK ON. Some people need a reality check, and here it is:

The amount of women in Computer Science is DECLINING. Wtf? There are so many sources that discuss the lack of women in tech that I have enough reading material for 2 lifetimes. Here's one from CNN about women being ignored in tech fields. Here's another (excellent) article about what it's like to be the first female anything (project manager, software lead, etc.):The Loneliness Of The Female Coder. Here's yet another from the New York Times that explains how and why women are out of the loop in Silicon Vally. Are there more, you say? Crain's Chicago Business discusses the lack of female role models and how it limits women's progress. NPR recently aired a segment that revealed that "Women-led businesses probably only receive between 5 and 10 percent of all the venture capital that's allocated to startups in their very earliest in their growth phases." A study was conducted to test why women get less funding. The results? ATROCIOUS.
